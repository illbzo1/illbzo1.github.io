I'm a writer.

I've been writing since I was 8 years old. Lately I've been writing long short stories. I'm told these are called "novelettes". I didn't know "novelette" was even a word until I finished one and a friend of mine clued me in.

I like video games. Like, more than a friend.

Strangely enough, despite being a dude who is huge into video games and will talk to you at length about his preferences in Pokemon (SPOILER ALERT: Ghosts) I don't consider myself a gamer. It's never been something I found to be a valuable part of my identity. I might as well call myself a "right-hander" or a "brown hair-er".

I'm into systems, not goals.

I want to make steady progress and constantly improve myself, not just hit specific metrics. I started off 2015 with these systems:

  • Write 500 words per day, and 1,000 on the weekends.
  • Read a book on writing every month.
  • Read a book on copywriting every month.
  • Read a book on marketing every month.

So far, these are working out well for me. My brain hasn't felt this rad in years.

I like making people laugh.

Nothing in life is better or more rewarding than the laughter of someone I care about.
