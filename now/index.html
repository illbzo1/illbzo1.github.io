What I'm Doing Now

This is what I'm doing lately. What's new in my life. It's a summary that isn't meant to replace a real conversation, but I'm sick of running into friends and struggling to answer the question "What've you been up to, man?"

This is what I've been up to, and this list was last updated on 1/16/2016.

Life

  1. I'm enjoying the current seasons of Adventure Time, Steven Universe, and Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles. Still working my way through Season 3 of Bob's Burgers, and Season 2 of Key and Peele.
  2. I'm reading Every House is Haunted by Ian Rogers. This book has been on my radar for a while, and it's been a fun read so far. As a writer of short, weird fiction, I absolutely love reading other short, weird fiction.
  3. I've been a lot more social lately. Between meetups, hanging out with friends, and meeting people, I'm spending more time around others. It's still draining (I don't think I'll ever be an extrovert) but draining in a good way, if that makes sense at all.

Work

  1. I've been working on some cool projects with a few clients. I've really enjoyed the work I've done in 2016 so far.
  2. I've also been meeting with a ton of cool people who are doing big things. Have I mentioned how much I love the tech scene here? I never felt at home in Indianapolis until I got involved with the Ruby community. It took me a few years, but I love living in Indy now.

Experiments

  1. I've been thinking a lot about my own daily happiness, after I finished reading Flow: The Psychology of Optimal Experience a couple weeks ago. I'm making a conscious effort to immerse myself in the task at hand, no matter what that task is, and it's making me happier and more productive. Even if the task is shoveling the driveway when it's 25 degrees outside.
  2. Speaking of productivity, I'm also keeping an eye on my own habits, and trying to identify things that are an obvious waste of time. I got really into a handful of iOS games over the holidays. I realized they were a near constant distraction, so I've deleted them. I've cleared my browsing history to keep me from checking in on time wasting websites. It's similar to not keeping junk food in the house. I can't trust myself not to eat all of it at once, so I just don't keep it around.

Writing

  1. Still working on finishing my first short story of the year. I've been slacking on my writing.
  2. However, I do have a short story submitted for publication, and I'm waiting to hear from the magazine.

Learning

  1. I'm (still) working through the Google Analytics Academy. I've been using Google Analytics for years, but most of my learning has been self-guided, or based on tutorials I've followed to solve a specific problem. I'm learning a lot through a dedicated, deep look at how Google Analytics works.
  2. I'm currently reading Trust Me, I'm Lying by Ryan Holiday. It's a great read, and it's made me even more skeptical of anything posted on a blog as "news".
  3. JavaScript January is well underway! I'm working through JavaScript and jQuery by Jon Duckett, and it's been fantastic. I've even been able to correct a handful of code typos as I go, and I'm much more comfortable with JavaScript than I was a couple weeks ago.
