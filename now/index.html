What I'm Doing Now

This is a list of what I'm doing lately. What's new in my life. It's a summary that can never replace a real conversation, but I'm sick of seeing a friend after time has passed, and struggling to answer the question "What've you been up to, man?"

This is what I've been up to, and this list was last updated on 11/1/2015.

Life

  1. Jessica and I are getting closer to adopting cats. We look at the Indianapolis Humane Society every day, but we haven't made the trip over there yet. I want kittens. She wants "established" cats. She wants to adopt two grandpas with tiny little strollers.
  2. I'm rewatching the Venture Brothers from Season 1. I'm currently working through Season 3. At this rate, I should be all caught up when Season 6 starts in 2016.
  3. I am actively enjoying the current seasons of Steven Universe, Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles, and Fargo. I am watching, though not exactly enjoying, the current season of American Horror Story.
  4. For the second year in a row, I am really into how beautiful the trees in Indiana look in the fall. I distinctly remember how I felt this time last year, when Jessica and I were house shopping. I think about everything that's happened since then, and I'm glad we're together, and I'm glad we are where we are. I am getting soft in my old age.
  5. I'm reading Down and Out in the Magic Kingdom. I've mostly been reading for professional development this year, so I'm making it a point to read more fiction.

Writing

  1. A few weeks ago, I sent a short horror story I wrote to 20 beta readers found through reddit.
  2. I've started implementing changes based on feedback from the 10 who responded.
  3. Once I've finished these edits, I'm going to try my hand at getting published in a horror magazine that accepts this type of story.
  4. I am aware of a weakness when it comes to editing my work. Currently, I have 12 short stories written. That's 8 first drafts, 3 second drafts, and 1 nearly finished draft (the story I mentioned earlier, sent to beta readers.) Publication is something I plan to pursue in the new year, so I am making a conscious effort to get better about editing my work. I don't have any illusions that all 12 of these stories are great, or even good, but they finished, and if they can be polished, I should polish them.

Learning

  1. I'm working through the Google Analytics Academy. I've been using Google Analytics for years, but most of my learning has been self-guided, or based on tutorials I've followed to solve a specific problem. I'm learning a lot through a dedicated, deep look at how Google Analytics works.
  2. I'm reading Hooked: How to Build Habit-Forming Products. This book's been on my radar for a while, and I'm really enjoying it so far.

Work

  1. I'm working with a few clients on their marketing and growth strategies.
  2. I'm also exploring other interesting career opportunities! Freelancing is fun, and I love the freedom, but long-term I want to be part of a team. I find I'm spending too much time wrangling clients, and not enough time spent doing the kind of data-driven marketing work I love.

Personal Development

  1. I'm running a handful of personal marketing experiments. I like to run experiments for a month or so - anything from seeing if answering marketing questions on Quora will drive traffic to my site to trying and measuring new channels for content distribution. I've found a month is a short enough time span that I don't lose focus, and long enough that I can evaluate an experiment for impact. I'm a huge fan of short feedback loops, too.
  2. I'm also working to improve my writing style (killing as many adverbs as possible, for starters!)
  3. I'm using Trello and Google Sheets to stay organized.
  4. I have recognized that my own education is not complete, and that it should never be complete. This was originally a Tweet, but I like it so much I'm putting it here. It's a good thing to think about.
