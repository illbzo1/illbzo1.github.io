What I'm Doing Now

This is a list of what I'm doing lately. What's new in my life. It's a summary that can never replace a real conversation, but I'm sick of seeing a friend after time has passed, and struggling to answer the question "What've you been up to, man?"

This is what I've been up to, and this list was last updated on 10/28/2015.

Life

  1. Jessica and I are getting closer to adopting cats. We look at the Indianapolis Humane Society every day, but we haven't made the trip over there yet. I want kittens. She wants "established" cats. She wants to adopt two grandpas with tiny little strollers.
  2. I'm rewatching the Venture Brothers from Season 1. At this rate, I should be all caught up when Season 6 starts in 2016.
  3. I am actively enjoying the current seasons of Steven Universe, Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles, and Fargo. I am watching, though not exactly enjoying, the current season of American Horror Story.
  4. For the second year in a row, I am really into how beautiful the trees in Indiana look in the fall. I remember distinctly how I felt this time last year, when Jessica and I were house shopping. I think about everything that's happened since then, and I'm glad we're together, and I'm glad we are where we are. I am getting soft in my old age.

Writing

  1. A few weeks ago, I sent a short horror story I wrote to 20 beta readers found through reddit.
  2. I've started implementing changes based on feedback from the 10 who responded.
  3. Once I've finished these edits, I'm going to try my hand at getting published in a horror magazine that accepts this type of story.

Learning

  1. I finished up the Ruby Koans on Tuesday, October 27. I wish I'd read this years ago. I still have trouble understanding how to write my own methods, but the Koans helped reinforce a lot of other things I understand about Ruby and programming in general.
  2. I'm reading Traction. Lots of great ideas about marketing products at multiple stages of their lifecycles.

Work

  1. I'm working with a few clients on their marketing and growth strategies.
  2. I'm also exploring other interesting career opportunities! Freelancing is fun, and I love the freedom, but long-term I want to be part of a team. Freelancing requires too much time spent wrangling clients, not enough time spent doing the kind of marketing work I love.

Personal Development

  1. I'm running a handful of personal marketing experiments.
  2. I'm also working to improve my writing style (killing as many adverbs as possible, for starters!)
  3. Every day is a struggle to stay organized. Trello and Google Sheets have proven to be fantastic project management tools.
  4. I recognize that people rarely act out of spite, but out of rational self-interest. (Which can seem like spite.)
