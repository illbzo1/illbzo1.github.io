What I'm Doing Now

This is a list of what I'm doing lately. What's new in my life. It's a summary that isn't meant to replace a real conversation, but I'm sick of running into friends and struggling to answer the question "What've you been up to, man?"

This is what I've been up to, and this list was last updated on 12/18/2015.

Life

  1. I'm playing both Fallout 4 and Bloodborne. I fully expect these to keep me busy well into the new year.
  2. I am actively enjoying the current seasons of Adventure Time, Jessica Jones, Steven Universe, and Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles. I am watching, though not exactly enjoying, the current season of American Horror Story. I wouldn't say I'm "hate watching" it exactly, but it definitely feels like I'm watching out of obligation.
  3. I am newly inducted into the world of transistors, LEDs, switches, and hardware hacking thanks to some friends. We've tentatively scheduled another weekend in early March, and I'm super excited.
  4. I'm reading more for pleasure. Next on my list is Starship Troopers, though I am lazy, and haven't actually started reading it yet. Maybe tonight!

Writing

  1. I have received my first official rejection from a magazine. It felt strangely good - like a rite of passage. I'm still looking into publication, and it's something I'll take a lot more seriously in 2016.
  2. I've rediscovered a love of reading physical books. Most of my reading this year has been on my iPad, and I'm making it a point to read more actual books now.

Learning

  1. I'm (still) working through the Google Analytics Academy. I've been using Google Analytics for years, but most of my learning has been self-guided, or based on tutorials I've followed to solve a specific problem. I'm learning a lot through a dedicated, deep look at how Google Analytics works.
  2. I'm currently reading The Thank You Economy. It's a good book about the value of sincerity (a common theme in many books I've read this year) and it's caused me to take a second look at the value I place on social media when it comes to marketing.

Work

  1. I'm working with a few clients on their marketing and growth strategies.
  2. I'm also exploring other interesting career opportunities! Freelancing is fun, and I love the freedom, but long-term I want to be part of a team. I find I'm spending too much time wrangling clients, and not enough time spent doing the kind of data-driven marketing work I love.

Professional Development

  1. I'm already working on a list of books to read next year. I've been thinking a lot about my career and where I want to go in the next year, the next five years, and the next ten, and what tools I'll need to get there.
  2. I'm running a handful of personal marketing experiments. I like to run experiments for a month or so - anything from seeing if answering marketing questions on Quora will drive traffic to my site to testing new channels for content distribution. I've found a month is short enough that I don't lose focus, and long enough I can evaluate an experiment for impact. I'm a huge fan of short feedback loops, too.
  3. I'm also working to improve my writing style (killing as many adverbs as possible, for starters!)
  4. I'm using Google Sheets, the Pomodoro Technique, and Trello to stay organized.
  5. I have recognized that my own education is not complete, and that it should never be complete. This was originally a Tweet, but I like it so much I'm putting it here. It's a good thing to think about.
