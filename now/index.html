What I'm Doing Now

This is a list of what I'm doing lately. What's new in my life. It's a summary that isn't meant to replace a real conversation, but I'm sick of running into friends and struggling to answer the question "What've you been up to, man?"

This is what I've been up to, and this list was last updated on 11/12/2015.

Life

  1. Jessica and I are getting closer to adopting cats. We look at the Indianapolis Humane Society every day, but we haven't made the trip over there yet. I want kittens. She wants "established" cats. She wants to adopt two grandpas with tiny little strollers.
  2. I am playing a ton of iOS games: Alphabear, Boom Beach, Clash of Clans, Final Fantasy Record Keeper, Monument Valley, and Neko Atsume. I've never been much for iOS games (aside from Sudoku) so this is a bit strange for me.
  3. I'm also playing Fallout 4. I've been a fan of the series since I picked up the first Fallout in an electronics store in my hometown back in 1998. Having a great time so far!
  4. I've been rewatching The Venture Brothers from the beginning for the last month or so. All I have left are the Halloween special, and "All This and Gargantua 2". It's funny how much I'd forgotten, but then again, there IS a two year break between each season.
  5. I am actively enjoying the current seasons of Adventure Time, Fargo, Steven Universe, and Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles. I am watching, though not exactly enjoying, the current season of American Horror Story. I wouldn't say I'm "hate watching" it exactly, but it definitely feels like I'm watching out of obligation.
  6. I'm reading Down and Out in the Magic Kingdom. I've mostly been reading for professional development this year, so I'm making it a point to read more fiction.

Writing

  1. I'm working on the final edit of a short story, after implementing feedback from several beta readers. The next step is to to try my hand at getting published in a horror magazine.
  2. I am aware of a weakness when it comes to editing my work. Currently, I have 12 short stories written. That's 8 first drafts, 3 second drafts, and 1 nearly finished draft. Publication is something I plan to pursue in the new year, so I'm making a conscious effort to get better about editing my work. I don't have any illusions that all 12 of these stories are great, or even good, but they're finished, and if they can be polished, I should polish them.
  3. I've rediscovered a love of reading physical books. Most of my reading this year has been on my iPad, and I'm making it a point to read more actual books now.

Learning

  1. I'm working through the Google Analytics Academy. I've been using Google Analytics for years, but most of my learning has been self-guided, or based on tutorials I've followed to solve a specific problem. I'm learning a lot through a dedicated, deep look at how Google Analytics works.
  2. I'm reading The Design of Everyday Things. It's been sitting around on my bookshelf for years, and I'm finally getting around to reading it.

Work

  1. I'm working with a few clients on their marketing and growth strategies.
  2. I'm also exploring other interesting career opportunities! Freelancing is fun, and I love the freedom, but long-term I want to be part of a team. I find I'm spending too much time wrangling clients, and not enough time spent doing the kind of data-driven marketing work I love.

Personal Development

  1. I'm running a handful of personal marketing experiments. I like to run experiments for a month or so - anything from seeing if answering marketing questions on Quora will drive traffic to my site to testing new channels for content distribution. I've found a month is short enough that I don't lose focus, and long enough I can evaluate an experiment for impact. I'm a huge fan of short feedback loops, too.
  2. I'm also working to improve my writing style (killing as many adverbs as possible, for starters!)
  3. I'm using Google Sheets, the Pomodoro Technique, and Trello to stay organized.
  4. I have recognized that my own education is not complete, and that it should never be complete. This was originally a Tweet, but I like it so much I'm putting it here. It's a good thing to think about.
